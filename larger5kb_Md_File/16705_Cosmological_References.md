## Subjects

The references below are organized into the following subjects:

  * Physical Constants
  * Classical Measurements of Cosmological Parameters
  * Large Scale Struture
  * Gravitational Lensing
  * Galaxy Formation
  * Cosmic Microwave Background
  * Primordial Nucleosynthesis
  * Inflation and Particle Physics
  * Statistics 

* * *

**Physical Constants**

* NIST's [ 1986 CODATA Recommended Values of the Fundamental Physical Constants](http://physics.nist.gov/PhysRefData/codata86/codata86.html) which appeared in Physics Today, August 1996. 

* Eric Weisstein's [ Eric's Treasure Trove of Astronomy](http://www.astro.virginia.edu/~eww6n/astro/astro.html) contains definitions and values of astronomical constants.

* Carlberg R. G., Yee H. K. C., Ellingson E., Abraham R., Gravel P., Morris S.  & Pritchet C. J. (1996) ``Galaxy Cluster Virial Masses and Omega'', [ ApJ, 462, 32](http://adsabs.harvard.edu/cgi-bin/nph-bib_query?1996ApJ%2E%2E%2E462%2E%2E%2E32C&db_key=AST).

* Chaboyer B., Demarque P., Kernan P. J. & Krauss L. M. (1997) ``The Age of Globular Clusters In Light of Hipparcos: Resolving the Age Problem'' ([astro-ph/9706128](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9706128)). 

* Gratton R. G., Fusi Pecci F., Carretta E., Clementini G., Corsi C. E., & Lattanzi M. G. (1997) ``Distances and Absolute Ages of Galactic Globular Clusters from Hipparcos Parallaxes of Local Subdwarfs'' ([astro-ph/9707107](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9707107)). 

* Creze M., Chereul E., Bienayme O., & Pichon C. (1997) ``The Distribution of Nearby Stars in Phase Space Mapped by Hipparcos: I. The Potential Well and Local Dynamical Mass'', A&A;, in press ([astro-ph/9709022](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9709022)). This paper concludes that the surface density of matter in the disk of the Milky Way near the Sun is 0.076 ¡À 0.015 solar masses pc-3, leaving no room for a disk-like component of Dark Matter, contradicting the established view dating back to Oort (1932).

**Cosmic Microwave Background**

* [ COBE DMR images](http://www.gsfc.nasa.gov/astro/cobe/dmr_image.html). 

* Holtzman J. A. (1989) ``Microwave Background Anisotropies and Large-Scale Structure om Universes with Cold Dark Matter, Baryons, Radiation, and Massive and Massless Neutrinos'' [ ApJS, 71, 1](http://adsabs.harvard.edu/cgi-bin/nph-bib_query?1989ApJS%2E%2E%2E71%2E%2E%2E%2E1H&db_key=AST). 

* The [ CMBFAST](http://arcturus.mit.edu:80/~matiasz/CMBFAST/cmbfast.html) code for computing linear CMB and matter fluctuation power spectra. The CMBFAST code is based in part on Ed Bertschinger's [ COSMICS](http://arcturus.mit.edu:80/cosmics/) package. For links to other CMB spectral codes, see `Other CMB links' near the bottom of the page of Max Tegmark's [ CMB data analysis center](http://www.sns.ias.edu/~max/cmb/experiments.html). 

* Hu W., Seljak U., White M.,  & Zaldarriaga M. (1997) ``A Complete Treatment of CMB Anisotropies in a FRW Universe'', Phys. Rev. submitted ([astro-ph/9709066](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9709066)). The final line of the abstract asserts, dramatically, ``With this work, the perturbation theory of CMB temperature and polarization anisotropy formation through the gravitational instability in an FRW universe may be considered complete''. This is a seriously good paper. 

* Zaldarriaga M., Seljak U. & Bertschinger E. (1997) ``Integral Solution for the Microwave Background Anisotropies in Non-Flat Universes'', ([astro-ph/9704265](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9704265)). 

* Lineweaver C. H. & Barbosa D. (1997) ``What Can Cosmic Microwave Background Observations Already Say About Cosmological Parameters?'', ([astro-ph/9706077](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9706077)). 

* Hancock S., Rocha G., Lasenby A. N. & Gutierrez C. M. (1997) ``Constraints on Cosmological Parameters from Recent Measurements of CMB Anisotropy'' ([astro-ph/9708254](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9708254)). 

* Challinor A. & Lasenby A. (1997) ``Recent Developments in the Calculation of CMB Anisotropies'' ([astro-ph/9711028](http://xxx.lanl.gov/abs/astro-ph/9711028)) on the covariant gauge-invariant formalism gives a taste of the edge of the envelope, for those who like edges of envelopes.

**Statistics**

* Wall J. V. (1996) ``Practical Statistics for Astronomers - II. Correlation, Data-modelling and Sample Comparison'', QJRAS, 37, 519-563. A readable introduction to various statistical tests, with a bunch of references. Not specifically geared to cosmologists. Page 550 states ``for the most part our ignorance of statistics... is profound'', but then generously footnotes ``Let me note honourable exceptions... there is a high level of awareness and very sophisticated use of statistical inference in large-scale structure studies''. Paper I of this series (Wall J. V. 1979, QJRAS, 20, 138) is the source of the quote ``Astronomers cannot avoid statistics, and there are several reasons for this unfortunate situation''.
