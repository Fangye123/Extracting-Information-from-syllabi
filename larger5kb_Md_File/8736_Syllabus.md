## Syllabus - Oral Health Outcomes DH 495

Northern Arizona University  
Department of Dental Hygiene  
DH 495 Oral Health Outcomes  
3 credit hours  
Prerequisite: DH 450  
Instructor: Tricia Moore / Ellen Grabarek  
BLDG 66, Room 206 Office hours by appointment  
[Tricia.Moore@nau.edu](mailto:Tricia.Moore@nau.edu) or phone 523-4012  
  
**Course Description:** The culminating experience in the Dental Hygiene
curriculum requiring synthesis of basic, behavioral and clinical sciences to
assess needs, plan goals and strategies, provide intervention and evaluate
oral health outcomes. Documented cases/projects become part of the
professional portfolio.  
  
**Objectives**  
， Read, critically analyze and discuss practical applications of scientific
literature.  
， Identify resources and obtain information needed to make evidenced-based
decisions.  
， Collect and analyze data to answer a research question or evaluate success
in meeting a community's oral health need.  
， Plan goals, priorities and strategies that facilitate oral health.  
， Provide appropriate, high quality and evidence-based interventions.  
， Measure the extent to which goals are achieved.  
， Determine appropriate  &QUOT;outcomes measure&QUOT; to evaluate success.  
， Evaluate cost-effectiveness of interventions provided.  
， Evaluate the ongoing oral health status of the individuals or communities
involved in the interventions.  
， When less than optimum results are obtained, determine potential reasons and
take appropriate steps.  
， Document a case through the use of photos, indices, narrative and other
creative means.  
， Apply principles of ethical behavior and professional conduct  
  
**Course Structure/ Activities**  
， Students will come to class prepared to make and/or discuss progress on
clinical, research and/or community cases.  
， Students are required to assess, plan, provide intervention, and evaluate
results of patient care and a research or community project  
， Students will prepare professional presentation of results including
photographs, clinical or epidemiological indices, narrative, statistics, and
other creative and appropriate approaches to documentation.  
  
Reading/resources: Required reading will be determined by project or patient
need. Students will be expected to identify and obtain a variety of resources
as indicated by individual cases.  
  
**Evaluation**  
， Tests and Assignments 30%  
， Self, peer and patient or client evaluations 10%  
， Learning Portfolio 10%  
..  Write a cover statement for your learning portfolio in which you explain
how the pieces in your portfolio link what you have learned in your major to
what you have learned in your liberal studies program. In this statement, you
should discuss your strengths and describe your goals for your future
development.  
Included in your learning portfolio will be:  
Your initial strenths/goals statement (UC101), pieces from your distribution
and Jr. Writing courses including a piece on ethics, annual self-assessment
statement of strengths and goals (DH Clinical Courses), clinical outcomes
analysis (DH495), research or community project report(DH495) and your cover
statement.

， Projects 50%  
Each student is required to do the following 3 projects.  
  
1\. Research or Community Project Report 20%  
2\. Self-improvement Project 15%  
3\. Clinical Outcomes Analysis 15%  
  
**Grading**

80-89% B

<65 F
