> #  Europe Since 1815  
>  HIST 2121  
>  T, TH 12:30-2:20  
>  Instructor: Prof. Tom Papademetriou  
>  Office Hours: K-236, Tuesdays 2:30-4:30  
>  and by appointment  
>  Tel: 748-6093 e-mail: [apapadem@stockton.edu](mailto:apapadem@stockton.edu)

>

> This course studies the development of modern European history from the
period of the French revolution to the present. While remaining mindful of
various approaches to studying the past, this course will use the political
historical narrative as the framework for understanding continuities and
change in the social and cultural history of Europe.

>

> Special emphasis will be placed upon the reading and discussion of primary
sources, so as to hear and contextualize the variety of  
>  voices that make up the historical record. It is expected that each student
comes to class prepared to discuss any of the primary  
>  source readings if called upon.

>

> Technology will be one of the essential tools upon which this class will
depend. The syllabus is available online with links to  
>  assignments, primary sources, and to the Web quizzes. Web quizzes must be
submitted electronically.

>

> Finally, the Term Paper (16-18 pages) for the course constitutes half of the
grade. Deadlines have been set to assist and facilitate the process. Meeting
each deadline is important both in preparing the Term Paper, as well as for
the grade. Due dates are: February 5: topic due, February 21: outline due,
March 7: historiographic essay due, April 11, Term Paper due.
